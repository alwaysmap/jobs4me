# Interview Prep Template

## Document Structure

```markdown
# Interview Prep: {Company} — {Role Title}

## Role Summary

{2-3 sentences: what the role is, what team it's on, what the mandate seems to be}

## Key Requirements and Your Stories

### {Requirement 1 from JD}

**Likely question**: "{How they'll probably ask about this}"

**Your story**: {Specific experience — name the company, the team, the outcome. Be concrete: numbers, timelines, scope. Pull from resume, portfolio, and additional_context.}

**Bridge**: {How this story connects to what they need. "At {company} I did X, which is directly relevant because you're dealing with Y."}

**Gap to address**: {If evidence is thin, how to frame it honestly. "I haven't done this at exactly this scale, but my experience with X at Y gave me the foundation for..."}

### {Requirement 2 from JD}

{Same structure}

### {Requirement 3 from JD}

{Same structure}

{Continue for each major requirement — typically 4-7 sections}

## Industry & Company Context Questions

{3-5 questions that come from who this company is — their industry, stage, and current moment — not just the JD}

### {Context topic — e.g., "Regulated environment" or "Scaling past PMF"}
**Why they'll ask**: {What about this company's situation makes this a likely topic}
**Likely question**: "{How they'll phrase it}"
**Your story**: {Mapped from user's evidence, same format as above}

### {Context topic 2}
...

## Behavioral Interview Prep

### Leadership / People Management
**Story to tell**: {Specific example of building, coaching, or restructuring a team}
**Numbers**: {Team size, growth, outcomes}

### Conflict / Difficult Stakeholder
**Story to tell**: {Specific example}
**Resolution**: {What happened, what you learned}

### Failure / Learning
**Story to tell**: {Honest example — pick one that shows growth}
**What changed**: {How this experience made you better}

## Questions to Ask Them

1. {Thoughtful question about the team/org structure}
2. {Question about the specific challenge this role was created to solve}
3. {Question about success metrics — "What does great look like in 6 months?"}
4. {Question about company strategy or recent moves — shows you've done research}
5. {Question about the team's current pain points}
6. {Optional: question about something from the company overview that genuinely interests you}
```

## Guidelines

- Every story should have a **specific company name, scope, and outcome**. "I led a cross-functional team" is weak. "At GitHub I led 35 TPMs across Copilot, platform availability, and security" is strong.
- Pull stories from the user's actual evidence — don't invent or embellish.
- If the user has a portfolio or blog, link to specific posts/projects that support a story.
- The "Gap to address" section is crucial. Interviewers appreciate honesty framed as self-awareness. "I haven't managed a 100-person org, but I built my team from 5 to 35 and navigated the challenges of that growth curve."
- Questions to ask should never be things easily answered by the company's website. They should demonstrate that you've done the research and are now going deeper.
